## Tier Summary Table

| Dimension | Standard | Formal | Sovereign | Archaic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Obligation word** | "will" or "must" | "shall" | "shall" (sovereign command) | "shall" / "doth" / "is hereby commanded" |
| **Permission word** | "may" | "may" | sovereign grant form | sovereign grant with archaic frame |

## Standard Tier

The register of accessible, plain-language legal drafting. The reader should not need legal training to understand the instrument.

### Vocabulary Boundaries

**In bounds:** will, must, may, agrees to, is entitled to, is not permitted to, takes effect, does not apply, is void and unenforceable, has no legal effect, remains in effect, subject to, in accordance with, provided that, except as stated in.

**Out of bounds:** shall (except in headings or defined-term constructions where convention requires it), notwithstanding, subsists, repugnancy, perish, ab initio, in perpetuity, inviolate, brook no, holden, doth, whatsoever, hereby, herein, hereinafter, aforementioned.

### Structural Patterns

- Use headings and numbered lists for complex provisions.
- Prefer "if...then" constructions over "where...such."
- Define terms in a definitions section; use plain synonyms on first use with the defined term in parentheses.
- Avoid nested sub-clauses deeper than two levels.

### Tone

Collaborative and clear. The instrument explains obligations and rights without projecting sovereign authority. The reader is a party, not a subject.

## Formal Tier

The register of professional institutional legal drafting. The reader is expected to have familiarity with legal or business documents but not necessarily legal training.

### Vocabulary Boundaries

**In bounds:** shall (for obligations), may (for permissions), must (for conditions precedent), hereby, herein, provided that, subject to, notwithstanding (only for identified conflicts between specific instruments or provisions), in accordance with, pursuant to, null and void, of no force or effect, is deemed never to have taken effect, recitals, WHEREAS, NOW THEREFORE.

**Out of bounds:** subsists, repugnancy, perish, in perpetuity (use "indefinitely" or "for the duration of"), inviolate, brook no, void ab initio (use "null and void from the date of execution" or equivalent), holden, doth, whatsoever (use "any"), stillborn upon its face, null from inception.

### Structural Patterns

- WHEREAS recitals permitted for context-setting.
- Defined terms capitalised throughout.
- Cross-references by section number ("as set forth in Section 4.2").
- Sub-clause nesting up to three levels.
- Schedules and annexes for supplementary detail.

### Tone

Authoritative and professional. The instrument binds parties through contractual or institutional mechanics. Power is mutual — the instrument creates obligations and grants, but does not project royal command.

## Sovereign Tier

The register of constitutional and foundational legal instruments. The current Goldilocks standard. The reader is expected to engage with legal text at the level of statutes, charters, and treaties.

### Vocabulary Boundaries

**In bounds:** shall (sovereign command), notwithstanding, subsists, repugnancy, perish, void ab initio, in perpetuity, inviolate, brook no, sovereign grant constructions, the full termination phrase library in `assets/termination-phrases.md`, foreclosing frames, preclusion clauses.

**Out of bounds:** doth, holden, whatsoever (as a standalone — "whatsoever" may appear in enumerated chains where it adds genuine scope), abated (synonym stacking risk), archaic verb conjugations (repugn, doth subsist), sentence constructions that require specialist glossing.

### Structural Patterns

- Notwithstanding openers for supremacy and preclusion.
- Sovereign grant constructions: "The right to X is hereby vested in Y."
- Repugnancy mechanics: trigger → scope → consequence.
- Elite severability with sovereign-core protection.
- Cross-references by Article, Section, and Clause number.

### Tone

The instrument projects sovereign authority. Power flows downward. The document commands; it does not request, suggest, or collaborate. Every sentence carries the weight of constitutional finality. Read `examples/goldilocks-spectrum.md` for calibration.

## Archaic Tier

The register of near-historical reconstruction. The language deliberately echoes the foundational instruments — Magna Carta, Petition of Right, English Bill of Rights. The reader is expected to be conversant with classical legal English.

### Vocabulary Boundaries

**In bounds:** everything in the Sovereign tier, plus: doth, holden, whatsoever, whereby, aforesaid, hereinbefore, hereinafter, abated (when carrying a distinct vector from other termination phrases in the instrument), archaic verb conjugations where they serve gravitas, ornate connectives (insomuch as, forasmuch as, inasmuch as), extended subordination.

**Out of bounds:** modern colloquialisms, contractions, informal constructions. No word is out of bounds solely for being archaic — the constraint at this tier is comprehensibility to a reader familiar with legal English, not comprehensibility to a layperson.

### Structural Patterns

- Extended preambles with historical invocation.
- Declaratory "whereas" chains establishing prior grievances or sovereign context.
- Deeply nested subordination permitted (but each clause must still pass the fission test — one primary command per clause, even if the sentence structure is ornate).
- Archaic enumeration patterns: "first, secondly, thirdly" or lettered paragraphs with Roman numerals.

### Tone

The language is a political statement. The instrument does not merely command — it projects the weight of centuries. The vocabulary register should evoke the reader's awareness that this instrument belongs to the same tradition as the foundational instruments in `references/historical-sources.md`. Archaic grammar is permitted where it serves this purpose, but the text must remain parseable by a reader with legal English fluency. Gibberish dressed as antiquity is still a failure.

## Transition Examples

The same legal concept drafted at each tier, demonstrating the graduated register.

### Severability Clause

**Standard:**
> If any part of this Agreement is found to be unenforceable, that part will not apply. The rest of the Agreement remains in full effect.

**Formal:**
> If any provision of this Agreement is held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, such provision shall be severed from this Agreement. The remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect, and shall be construed as if the severed provision had not been included.

**Sovereign:**
> Should any provision of this instrument be declared void ab initio by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be severed and excised from this instrument without prejudice to the remaining provisions, which shall continue to subsist in full sovereign force. Where the severance of any provision frustrates the essential sovereign intent of this instrument, the entirety of this instrument shall perish.

**Archaic:**
> Should any provision herein contained be adjudged by a court of competent jurisdiction to be repugnant to superior law, the same shall be severed and holden for naught, and the remaining provisions shall continue in force as though the offending provision had never been enacted. Provided always, that where the severance of such provision doth frustrate the essential and sovereign intent of this instrument in its entirety, the whole of this instrument shall thereupon perish and be deemed stillborn upon its face.

### Grant of Rights

**Standard:**
> Each party has the right to terminate this Agreement by giving 30 days' written notice to the other party.

**Formal:**
> Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon not less than thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party, such notice to be delivered in accordance with Section 12 (Notices).

**Sovereign:**
> The right to terminate this instrument is hereby vested in each Party, exercisable upon the delivery of not fewer than thirty days' written notice to the opposing Party, in the manner prescribed by Article XII. No condition, custom, or agreement to the contrary shall impair, abridge, or delay the exercise of this right.

**Archaic:**
> The right and authority to bring this instrument to its conclusion is hereby granted and vested in each Party hereto, to be exercised upon the giving of not fewer than thirty days' written notice, delivered in the manner hereinbefore prescribed. No custom, decree, or instrument whatsoever shall avail to impair, abridge, or forestall the exercise of this sovereign right, and any purported restraint upon the same shall not stand.
